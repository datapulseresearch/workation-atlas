# Workation Atlas 2026

Open dataset behind the study **[Workation Atlas 2026: the best workation cities for Germans, ranked three ways](https://www.datapulse.de/en/workation-atlas)** by DataPulse Research.

The study ranks workation destinations for German remote workers in three separate ways, because the best city depends on employment status: freelancers chasing adventure, value-maximisers, and employees restricted to the EU.

## Key findings

- **Germany accounts for 52 %** of all global "workation" searches, by far the largest share worldwide.
- There is no single best workation city. The top pick differs by traveller type: **Bangkok** (Adventure Seeker), **Jaipur** (Smart Spender) and **Seville** (Easy Mover / EU-only employees).
- Asia dominates the freelancer rankings, while EU/Southern Europe wins for employees bound by social-insurance and tax rules.

## The three rankings

- **Adventure Seeker** (freelancers & founders): Bangkok, Kuala Lumpur, Chiang Mai, Da Nang, Ubud.
- **Smart Spender** (best value per euro): Jaipur, Bangkok, Da Nang, Chennai, Weligama.
- **Easy Mover** (corporate employees, EU-only): Seville, Ericeira, Valencia, Faro, Rome.

## Methodology

- **Rankings** weight climate, cost of living, flight prices, remote-work infrastructure, safety and (for employees) EU/EFTA membership differently per traveller type.
- **Search demand** measured via Ahrefs global keyword data for "workation".
- **Source:** DataPulse Research; search data from Ahrefs.
